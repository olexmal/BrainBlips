# Critical Success Factors

:::danger Security & Safety
The MCP servers **must** be the gatekeepers, validating all actions against business rules before execution.
:::

## Key Success Factors

### 1. Security & Safety
The MCP servers must validate all actions against business rules, ensuring no unauthorized or unsafe operations reach production networks.

### 2. Human-in-the-Loop
:::warning Approval Required
Mandate confirmation for all changes. Position Claude as an expert assistant, not an autonomous system.
:::

- All write operations require explicit approval
- Multi-level confirmation for critical changes
- Clear audit trails for all actions

### 3. Data Quality
The AI is only as good as its knowledge base and network data. 

**Requirements:**
- Clean and structured documentation
- Up-to-date equipment manuals
- Accurate network topology data
- Historical incident records

### 4. Change Management
Train senior specialists to "teach" the AI and new staff to trust and use it effectively.

**Action Items:**
- Knowledge capture sessions with senior experts
- Regular AI system updates and improvements
- User training programs
- Feedback loops for continuous improvement

## Conclusion

This initiative transforms a major constraint into a strategic advantage. 

The client is not just replacing retiring experts; they are building a scalable system that:
- ✅ Institutionalizes knowledge
- ✅ Accelerates onboarding
- ✅ Improves operational efficiency
- ✅ Increases development velocity

The MCP-based AI Copilot is a pragmatic and powerful solution to a critical industry problem.
